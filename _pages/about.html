People

Juliette Cezzar is a designer, educator, and writer. Her design practice has created award-winning experiences across digital and physical media for arts, hospitality, media, museum, and education clients. She recently returned to her role as a tenured professor at Parsons School of Design after a year and a half serving clients on multidisciplinary teams at McKinsey Design. As a design leader and mentor, she has served as director of Communication Design at Parsons School of Design and President of AIGA NY. She holds a Bachelor of Architecture from Virginia Tech and an MFA in Graphic Design from Yale University.

Jeremy Zilar works with artists, musicians, writers, poets, creators, foundations and mission-driven organizations to improve the their unique rhythms, business operations, and artistic process. In response to the Covid-19 pandemic, he helped The Brooklyn Rail produce The New Social Environment, a series of daily conversations with artists and poets. He spent four years as strategist at 18F and Director of Digital.gov, the U.S. Web Design System, PlainLanguage.gov and 22 communities of practice. Formerly, he oversaw the blogs at The New York Times and launched over 220 blogs, with some of the most talented writers in the world. All of his work is underpinned by deep hands-on expertise in front- and back-end engineering and systems design, connected to his materials-based art practice.
